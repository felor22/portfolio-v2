Directed and edited promotional videos for an on-site holistic health clinic

Client: Te Manu Aute Whare Oranga - Holistic Health Clinic

Challenge

Te Manu Aute Whare Oranga offers community members a variety of free health services, from general practitioners to traditional healers. However not enough members of the community were taking advantage of or even aware of these free services. My task was to to create short promotional videos that show the breadth and diversity of services offered by the clinic and their unique holistic approach.

Process

Initially I held stakeholder interviews with the manager and staff of Te Manu Aute to discuss the goals of the clinic and their primary target audience. I then met with each practitioner and healer to better understand their services and worldview. Since I was living and working in the same community, I was also able to informally speak with people in the area and ask about their opinions on and experiences with the clinic.

From my research it became clear that not only was there a lack of awareness in the community, but also hesitancy towards trying unfamiliar traditional healing services.

I decided to create short, visually descriptive videos for each practitioner and healer that would actually show and explain their therapy. I also created a longer overview video and an additional clip with community testimonials so that people could hear about the positive impact of these services from their peers.

After creating a story arc for each video and coordinating all of the interviews, I worked with a videographer and directed the filming, which I later edited using Final Cut Pro.

Results

Over the course of two months I planned, directed and edited a total of 11 videos, which were uploaded to the clinic’s website, broadcasted on televisions in the waiting room, and shared on social media. My initial ethnographic research enabled me to create videos that captured the mission and goals of the holistic health clinic while being informative and enjoyable to watch for the community members.

After the release of these videos Te Manu Aute Whare Oranga saw a marked increase in the number of people who signed up for their services.
